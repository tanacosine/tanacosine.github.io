The ECG Handbook

The ECG Handbook was something that I procured during my general clerkship as part of my set of notes before the (then) mid-block Data Interpretation assessment. I used some experience from my time back then working as a collaborator within the Faculty ECG Handbook to help me better procure this piece. The handbook features typical findings of different types of cardiac conditions, with a special emphasis on the presentation of arrhythmias and their pathomechanisms. Definitely helped me pass the mid-block without a hitch, and has been distributed to others before being superceded by my pocket guide.

[Incomplete] ECG Pocket Guide

This was made after my personal ECG Handbook following feedback from my peers. The most commonly heard concern was that there were not enough annotated ECG strips, and descriptions bunched up within the ECG Handbook did little to help students pinpoint their corresponding findings on the strips themselves. The feedback from the ECG handbook were incorporated into this pocket guide, which focuses on helping readers visualise the specific waves that the descriptive terms are pointing to. Given the heavy annotations that are often required, as well as my current workload as an Assistant Intern, this project remains incomplete. However, I will try to make time to work on this during brief reprieves from studying, and will upload a completed version as soon as it is available.

Preclinical Cardiac Cycle & ECG NPT
